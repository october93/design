Recommendations from the Editors
“Judicious choice of training examples is necessary for improving reinforcement learning policies. With GANs, we train the generator and discriminator simultaneously and with games we utilize self-play. Reverse curriculum generation finds Starts of Intermediate Difficulty for which obtaining the reinforcement reward becomes progressively harder.”
Reverse Curriculum Generation for Reinforcement Learning Agents
December 20, 2017
Read the article at berkeley.edu
“Tom B. Brown et al. have figured out how to print physical patches that, when added to a scene, cause machine classifiers to mistake anything for a toaster.”
Adversarial Patch
December 27, 2017
Read the paper at arxiv.org
“Jiawei Su et al. use differential evolution to identify single-pixel changes to images which cause machine classifiers to misidentify them with very high average confidence.”
One pixel attack for fooling deep neural networks
October 24, 2017
Read the paper at arxiv.org
“Tim Kraska et al. posit that existing index structures can be replaced with deep-learning models, dubbed learned indexes. Using neural nets trained to effectively predict the position or existence of records, they outperform cache-optimized B-Trees by up to 70% in speed and an order-of-magnitude in memory usage over various data sets.”
The Case for Learned Index Structures
December 4, 2017
Read the paper at arxiv.org
“Turing Learning augments GANs by allowing discriminators to control stimuli affecting both the training data and generator distributions, offering an advantage analogous to a game of 20 Questions. Groß et al. show the advantages by teaching a robot to infer its own sensor configuration through controlled movements.”
Generalizing GANs: A Turing Perspective
December 04, 2017
